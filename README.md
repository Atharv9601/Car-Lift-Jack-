# CAR LIFT JACK (MECHANICAL JACK DESIGN) 🧰🚗

## 1. Overview

The **Car Lift Jack** project focuses on designing a compact mechanical lifting mechanism capable of safely raising a vehicle to a serviceable height.  
The emphasis is on **load‑bearing structure, kinematics, and safety**, while ensuring the design is manufacturable and easy to operate.

---

## 2. Aim 🎯

- Design a **mechanical jack** that can lift a passenger car within a defined **load capacity** (e.g., 1–1.5 tons).  
- Ensure the mechanism is **stable, self‑locking (or safely restrained)**, and ergonomically operable by one person.  
- Create a CAD model that is **manufacturing‑aware**, with realistic material thicknesses, welds/fasteners, and contact surfaces.

## 4. Mechanism & Components 🧩

Typical components in the car lift jack concept:

- **Base plate**  
  - Contacts the ground, spreads load, and prevents sinking/tilting.  
  - May include ribs or flanges for stiffness.

- **Upper saddle / lifting pad**  
  - Interfaces with the car’s jacking point.  
  - Designed to avoid slipping and to distribute local stress.

- **Link arms / scissor members** (for a scissor‑type jack)  
  - Crossed arms forming the main lifting structure.  
  - Connected via pivot pins or bolts with bushings/washers.

- **Lead screw / actuating screw**  
  - Central threaded element converting rotational input into lift.  
  - Designed with appropriate **pitch and length** for the required stroke and mechanical advantage.

- **Handle or drive input**  
  - Foldable handle or hex drive for applying torque.  
  - Positioned to give adequate leverage without colliding with the car body.

- **Pins, bushings, and hardware**  
  - Joints designed with realistic diameters, clearances, and retention (circlips, nuts, etc.).

---

## 5. Modelling Workflow 🧱

1. **Define requirements**  
   - Target load (e.g., 1–1.5 tons).  
   - Lift height range (minimum collapse height, maximum lift height).  
   - Available space under vehicle and jack point dimensions.

2. **Kinematic layout**  
   - Choose mechanism type (scissor jack is typical for compact designs).  
   - Determine **link lengths, pivot positions, and screw stroke** to achieve the required lift.

3. **CAD modelling**  
   - Model base, arms, saddle, and screw with parametric dimensions.  
   - Add fillets, chamfers, and ribs where needed for stress reduction and stiffness.

4. **Assembly & motion check**  
   - Assemble all components with constraints.  
   - Simulate jack extension/collapse to check:
     - No interference between links.  
     - No collision with base, saddle, or ground.

5. **Detailing & documentation**  
   - Create **2D drawings** with key dimensions and tolerances.  
   - Generate a basic **BOM** for all parts and hardware.

---

## 6. Key Features & Engineering Highlights 🌟

- **Mechanical advantage & ergonomics**  
  - Screw pitch and handle length chosen so that a single user can lift the vehicle with reasonable effort.

- **Stability & safety**  
  - Wide enough base and proper geometry to keep the jack stable under load.  
  - Contact interfaces designed to minimise slipping at both ground and vehicle ends.

- **Manufacturing‑ready geometry**  
  - Members designed as **sheet metal or simple machined sections**, suitable for welding, bending, and drilling.  
  - Avoids overly complex shapes that would be hard or expensive to produce.

- **Service‑friendly design**  
  - Replaceable pins and screws.  
  - Clear access to lubrication points and threads.
